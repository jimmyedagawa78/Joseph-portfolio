Case Closed: Scarlet Bullet Makes its Way to America

By Joseph Blessing Stephen · 2 min read · October 2021

After a successful worldwide premiere of Detective Conan: The Scarlet Bullet in various countries around the world — from Japan all the way to France — the 24th movie in the Detective Conan franchise (also known as Case Closed) has made its way to the United States.

The movie will be screened at Anime NYC on November 21st, 2021. The website states: "Join us for the world premiere of English dub of Case Closed: Scarlet Bullet, Detective Conan's latest cinematic adventure! Trivia contest with prizes and a Q&A with the English voice cast to follow."

A MILESTONE FOR THE FRANCHISE

This marks a significant moment for the Detective Conan franchise in the West. While the series has enjoyed massive popularity across Asia and Europe for decades, its English-language presence has historically been limited. A theatrical premiere at one of North America's biggest anime conventions signals a growing appetite for the franchise among Western audiences.

The Scarlet Bullet connects to the ongoing storyline of the franchise while standing on its own as a thrilling mystery — a balance the series has always done well. For fans who have followed the English dub, this theatrical event is a rare chance to experience the film on the big screen with the full English cast.

Seeing Detective Conan reach American theatres is a moment fans have been waiting a long time for.

For those who haven't yet dived into the franchise, this is as good a starting point as any. The Conan films are largely accessible as standalone mysteries, making them a great entry point into one of anime's most enduring series.
