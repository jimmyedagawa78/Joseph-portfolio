Life is Strange: True Colors — A Meaningful Successor to the Franchise

By Joseph Blessing Stephen · 2 min read · 2021

Life is Strange: True Colors is a meaningful addition to the Life is Strange franchise — taking a step back from a vast road trip and focusing on the small town of Haven Springs. While nothing can compare to Max's time-bending powers, Alex's empathy abilities give the game its heart and help flush out the residents of Haven Springs in a way that feels genuinely earned.

HAVEN SPRINGS FEELS ALIVE

The decision to set the entire game in one location pays off. Haven Springs is a small Colorado mountain town with a tight-knit community, and the game gives you time to actually know these people before the larger story asks you to care about them. That's a structural choice that earlier entries in the franchise sometimes rushed past.

Alex's power — the ability to feel and absorb the emotions of others — is used both as a gameplay mechanic and as a narrative device. When you absorb someone's fear or grief, you feel the weight of it, and the game doesn't let you shake it off quickly. It's a thoughtful use of the power fantasy that Life is Strange has always played with.

HOW IT STACKS UP

When comparing True Colors to other installments in the Life is Strange franchise, the original remains the best — Max and Chloe's story set a bar that's hard to match. But True Colors comes closer than most. It doesn't try to replicate what came before; it finds its own emotional register and commits to it.

True Colors is proof that Life is Strange still has meaningful stories left to tell.

If you've been on the fence about this one — especially if Before the Storm or Life is Strange 2 didn't fully land for you — True Colors is worth your time. It's a quieter game, but a genuinely moving one.
