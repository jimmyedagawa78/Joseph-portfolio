Our First Look at Netflix's Live-action Cowboy Bebop

By Joseph Blessing Stephen · 1 min read · August 2021

We have finally received our first look at the live-action adaptation of the beloved iconic anime Cowboy Bebop from Netflix. Netflix has released a slew of images featuring the cast of the live-action remake.

Seeing these images gives hope to fans of the franchise that this may not be a repeat of the Death Note adaptation — particularly with the return of composer Yoko Kanno from the original series, and the director consulting with creator Hajime Yatate.

WHY THIS ONE FEELS DIFFERENT

Live-action anime adaptations have a notoriously mixed track record in the West. Death Note, Dragon Ball Evolution, and Ghost in the Shell have all stumbled in different ways — either by stripping the source of its cultural specificity, or by misunderstanding what made the original work in the first place.

Cowboy Bebop is a harder case. The original 1998 series is not just beloved — it's considered a benchmark of the medium. Its jazz-infused soundtrack, its episodic storytelling, and its deeply melancholic tone are all things that are notoriously difficult to translate.

Yoko Kanno returning to score the live-action is the single most reassuring piece of news this production could have delivered.

The visual language of the images released so far suggests a production that has at least studied the source carefully. Whether that translates to a faithful adaptation, time will tell — but the foundation looks more promising than most.
