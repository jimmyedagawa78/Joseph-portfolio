Twelve Minutes Review: Twelve Minutes for the Ages

By Joseph Blessing Stephen · 1 min read · 2021

Twelve Minutes is an idea 10+ years in the making — and it shows. It is a refreshing take on the time-loop genre of games, with a compelling story of a man reliving the same 12 minutes of his life over and over again.

As you play, you get to know the characters one loop at a time. The game leaves everything at your disposal (except the stove) to use and further the plot. As the loops go on and the story unravels, you start to piece the puzzle together.

LOOP BY LOOP

The top-down perspective is an unusual choice for a narrative game, but it works. It creates a sense of watching a scene play out like a theatre performance — you're both in the story and observing it. Every item in the apartment is a potential clue or tool, and the game rewards experimentation.

Twelve Minutes gives you just enough rope to figure things out yourself — and just enough mystery to keep pulling you back in.

The voice cast — featuring James McAvoy, Daisy Ridley, and Willem Dafoe — elevates the material considerably. There are some parts of the story that stretch credibility, but the core loop is compelling enough to carry you through to the end.

If you enjoy narrative puzzle games and don't mind the discomfort of a story that refuses to give you easy answers, Twelve Minutes is well worth your time.
